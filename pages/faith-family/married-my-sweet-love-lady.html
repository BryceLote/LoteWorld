Married My Sweet Love Lady

Written eight days after our wedding
August 11, 2024

The wedding went perfect. Alli planned everything so well.

Nehemiah and I spent the morning watching Atlantis: The Lost Empire, and then hung out at the house and ate charcuterie with Ethan and Gage until it was time to get ready.

Nehemiah and I wore suits. Mine was all red and his was grey with a pink bowtie and pocket square. While we got cleaned up and ready, he and I spoke about what it means for our family when I marry his mother and how excited I am to be his Dad. It is important to me to communicate to him that I am choosing to be his father, and that I could not have chosen a better son. He was very well behaved and genuinely excited for the wedding. When it came time to leave, he and I rode with Uncle Gage in his truck, and he did not even ask to bring anything with him to occupy himself during the ride. He did fall asleep on the way, which was a good thing.

· · ·

When we arrived at The Olie Gathering Place, we were not yet allowed inside because Alli and her wedding party were still getting ready for the ceremony. It was hot outside and we were all dressed in layers, but it wasn't too bad considering the shade and breeze the venue provided. This gave us time to prepare for the ceremony with Dad and get the speaker situation figured out, so that Dad could put the mic in his front pocket while officiating. After that, we all hung out with Aunt Tammy and Uncle Brian until it was time for my wedding party to take pictures with the photographer, Ashley.

Taking wedding pictures with the guys and Nehemiah was honestly a really good time. Everyone was engaged and I personally enjoyed working with Ashley. She directed everyone super well and I suspect she got some amazing shots.

Shortly after we finished shooting, guests started arriving, probably around 4:30 to 4:45 PM. I chatted with most people as they came in. At 5:00 guests promptly found their seats and eagerly awaited the beginning of the ceremony. During this time Dad became a little anxious and was more than ready to get the show on the road.

· · ·

Finally, around 5:15 to 5:20, it was show time.

This is where I began to feel really emotional. Throughout the entire day I can say confidently that I was not nervous or anxious until this moment. It was finally time to marry the woman of my dreams and become husband and wife. Norah and I were second to walk down the aisle, behind Mom and Dad. I have to take note of how lucky I am to be blessed with the opportunity to walk with my daughter down the aisle toward the formal unification of our family. Following us were Ethan and Angie, next Gage and Cheyanne, then finally Nehemiah and Alli.

From this moment on in the ceremony, all I could see was Alli. Once she came around the corner, my eyes welled up with tears and I could see nothing but her. She met me at the altar in front of our families, friends, and God.

Dad began officiating the wedding, but all I could focus on was Alli and the overwhelming feeling of love I was experiencing in that moment. Apparently the catering truck had arrived during the ceremony and caused distractions, but I never noticed. I remained on cloud nine until it was time to say our I Do's and share our public vows. We shared our first kiss as man and wife, the music came on just as planned, and then we walked away from the altar as a married couple. At the end of the row, in front of everyone, I dipped Alli once more and kissed her again before leaving the ceremony completely.

· · ·

After the ceremony is where the real tears began.

Everyone went back inside and gave Alli and me the opportunity to share our private vows with one another. We returned to the altar where we had just been married to open our envelopes and make our vows to one another. While the ceremony was beautiful, this was by far the most special moment for me. I shared mine with Alli first. As we shared with one another, I was overcome with joy. The depth of our love and the realization of what we had done became clear to me in this moment. We vowed to be with one another forever after this moment.

Once we completed our vows, we ordered our food and went in to dance our first dance as a married couple. Neither of us had ever danced before, but we decided to perform a choreographed dance to the song "Good in Me" by Andy Grammer. The song resonated with us both tremendously and we thought it would be an appropriate way to set the tone for our marriage. Together we would overcome our fears and express ourselves in dance to those we invited to share this occasion with us. Although Alli's dress was very large, the dance went well, and I can say with confidence that we both enjoyed ourselves thoroughly.

· · ·

The reception had officially begun.

Alli and I sat in the center of the room and the best man and maid of honor brought us our pizza. This pizza was very special because our first date was at a local Neapolitan pizza restaurant called The Saucee Sicilian, who catered our wedding. They cooked all of our guests their very own pizzas and brought Alli's favorite lemon curd cheesecake, as well as two varieties of cannoli: chocolate chip and pistachio. Shortly after finishing our pizzas, we cut the cheesecake as a couple and began the dessert portion of our meal.

For the remainder of the night we mingled with our guests and danced to the playlist Angie had compiled for the occasion.

Everything had gone perfectly until about this point. Originally, it was planned that the kids would ride home with and stay the night with Alli's mom, who had been staying in a hotel room in town. However, it was discovered that Memaw had snuck alcohol into the wedding and had been secretly drinking wine coolers throughout the evening. She had fully intended on driving our kids to her hotel that night after drinking. Once we were made aware of this, the alcohol was immediately disposed of and plans were changed so that our kids would instead go home with my parents and spend the night at their house. The kids were of course crushed by this news and were filled with feelings of betrayal and abandonment following their Memaw's decisions.

Apart from this, the wedding was a complete success, due to the hard work and support of both our close friends and family. We ended up having such a great time that we stayed later than most of our guests. We departed from the venue around 8:30 PM and ordered sushi to be delivered on our way home.

Alli and I got home, cleaned ourselves up, and enjoyed a secret sushi date as husband and wife, thus concluding what I consider to be the best day of my life so far.
