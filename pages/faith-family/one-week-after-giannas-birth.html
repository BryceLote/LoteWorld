One Week After Gianna's Birth

Gianna, born September 6, 2025 at 12:58 AM · 8 lbs 7 oz · 20½ inches
Written one week later
~3 min read

It's been a week since Gianna came into our lives, and I still can't fully wrap my head around everything that's happened. The birth itself feels like the most profound moment of my life. Watching Alli bring her into the world, and being present for every wave of pain, fear, and breakthrough, has changed me forever. I didn't know what to expect, and honestly, it didn't feel real until I was in it. All I could do was hold space for Alli, help her release what she was carrying, and be there through each obstacle.

When she finally let go and chose the epidural, it felt like a turning point. The room shifted. Labor became bearable, even joyful. We could breathe and actually experience each moment instead of just surviving it. But then the scare after Gianna was born, the sudden rush of staff, the fear in my chest that something might go wrong; it was terrifying. That moment is still lodged inside me, a reminder of how fragile it all is.

Through it all, my love for Alli grew in ways I didn't think were possible. I thought I already knew the depth of it, but when I saw Gianna's little body enter this world, I could physically feel my heart expand. Like there was more room inside me than I ever imagined. I cry every time I look back at the photos and videos. I just can't believe this is real.

Gianna, she is perfect. I didn't expect to feel such an immediate bond. It took only a few hours for it to really sink in that she's ours, but since then, it's been overwhelming in the best way. She came from our flesh, and I can feel that in the deepest parts of me. I will always be there for her. I already know that.

Norah has stepped into her role as big sister with so much excitement, though it's brought out her bossiness toward Nehemiah. She wants to help, and I love that about her. Nehemiah is still figuring it out; I can tell he feels the love but doesn't quite know what to do with it yet. I've been doing my best to keep showing up for both of them, to make sure they still feel seen, but it's a juggling act. Gianna needs so much right now, and she is completely helpless without us.

As for me, I'm exhausted, but my heart is full. I feel like the most "Dad" I've ever been. Gianna's vulnerability has cracked me open in a new way; she reminds me what it means to put someone else first, completely. At the same time, I'm anxious about going back to work. I hate the idea of losing this sacred time, of slipping back into night shifts and missing the best moments of these early days. That transition weighs on me.

I have faith, though. Faith that God will guide us through this new season. The future feels uncertain, but there's also this sense of trust that we'll find our rhythm as a family of five. It won't be perfect, but it will be ours.

Right now, I just want to hold onto this feeling, the intensity of love, the awe, the gratitude, even the fear, because it means I'm alive, it means I'm present, and it means I've been given a gift beyond anything I ever dreamed.
