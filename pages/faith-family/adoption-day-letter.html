Letter to Norah and Nehemiah

Written at 2:55 AM, the morning of adoption day
October 24, 2025

My sweet Norah and Nehemiah,

Today is a very special day; one that makes something official that has already been true in my heart for a long time. From this day forward, by law and by name, you are my children. But in every way that matters, you've been my son and daughter since the moment I first chose you, and I will continue to choose you every single day of my life.

This moment is more than a piece of paper. It's a promise, a commitment that can never be broken. It means that no matter what the world brings, you belong, you are safe, and you are loved as my children, completely and forever.

· · ·

Norah, my caring, thoughtful, and radiant girl. You love so big, and your heart has room for everyone around you. From the very beginning, you opened that big heart to me without hesitation. You've shown me what it means to love unconditionally, to welcome someone in, and to believe in family. I am so proud of who you are; your kindness, your strength, and your joy. I hope you always remember that you are chosen, cherished, and deeply loved. You can always count on me when life feels hard, when you need a safe place, and when you just want to celebrate something beautiful. I will always be here.

· · ·

Nehemiah, my builder, my thinker, my dreamer. You see the world in such a special way, and it's been an honor watching you grow into the young man you are becoming. You've learned what it means to protect, to care, and to create things that last. I've loved every moment we've shared building worlds together, lifting each other up, talking about God, and learning side by side. You're growing into a man of strength, respect, and compassion, and I pray you always know your worth, guard your heart wisely, and continue to love with courage. I'm proud to be your dad and to walk beside you as you become everything God created you to be.

· · ·

Today brings me joy and peace; joy because our family is whole, and peace because I know that no matter what happens, we are bound together forever. You are my children in name, in love, and in legacy.

I love you both with everything I am. You are my greatest blessings, and I will never stop being the father you deserve.

Love always,
Dad
