The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind Foundation 10
3000+ hrs · Open World RPG · 2002

Played thousands of hours starting around age 7, before I could read fluently. Completed the game through spatial memory, pattern recognition, and world-reading rather than text. All-time favorite. The game that established how I process complex systems — by inhabiting them

The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion Foundation 9
600+ hrs · Open World RPG · 2006

600+ hours, most of it modded. One of the first games I could mod and run on my own machine. The vanilla systems are frustrating, but that was almost the point; it functioned more as a sandbox for understanding how games work than as a game I played straight. I experimented with small tweaks of my own and tried a ton of other people's mods. It opened my eyes to what was possible.

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim Hall of Fame 8
234.9 hrs · Open World RPG · 2011

470 hours total across both editions. One of the defining games. Deep RPG investment, world-immersion.

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt Hall of Fame 8
200 hrs · Open World RPG · 2015

Quality-wise this should be a 9 or 10. The world feels more alive and lived-in than any game I've played since. Every quest feels real and important, and the player isn't the center of the universe, just another inhabitant of the world. Great build variety, phenomenal story, and the graphics pushed me to build a new PC which is another favorite hobby. The reason it's not higher is my own inability to commit to long story games as I've gotten older. I used to fully immerse in multi-dozen-hour campaigns. Now I play 10-20 hours, get busy, come back feeling lost, and end up starting over instead of pushing through. I've completed it once and played 30-40 hours into separate saves multiple times. I've experienced most of what it has to offer. The rating reflects me, not the game.

Fallout 4 Hall of Fame 7
162.1 hrs · Open World RPG / FPS · 2015

My least favorite Fallout game. I spent hundreds of hours on 3 and New Vegas on PS3 and was very excited for this one. Got it at release, spent a ton of time exploring the wasteland and even more time building bases. But the story is weak and the roleplaying elements are shallow. I haven't even completed the main story despite many attempts. It's a good action RPG but not a great Fallout game.

DARK SOULS III Hall of Fame 9
256.2 hrs · Action RPG / Soulslike · 2016

The first Dark Souls that actually clicked. I had tried DS1 earlier and bounced off it because it was so different from the RPGs I knew. DS3 got popular and something about it landed. Played it for a while, didn't finish, went back to DS1 and fell in love with the series there. Came back to DS3 with a fresh save and completed it. Built multiple characters for PvP, which pulled me into collecting nearly all items and endings in the process. DS1 is the favorite, but DS3 is the one that caught the deepest hyperfixation. Played it extensively for months and really haven't been back since.

ELDEN RING Hall of Fame 9
164.6 hrs · Action RPG / Soulslike / Open World · 2022

The most refined version of the Souls formula. Does everything I love about the DS games; character building, world building, and both the levels and bosses are top notch. The only reason I don't play it more is the same thing that makes it great. It's so vast that if I come back after time away I feel lost and disconnected. Same friction I have with long games generally, just applied to the best version of my favorite genre.

DARK SOULS: REMASTERED Hall of Fame 10
129.4 hrs · Action RPG / Soulslike · 2018

242 hours combined across PTDE and Remastered. One of my all-time favorites. The game that defined the Soulslike for me — failure as a learning mechanism, mastery through repetition, a world that trusts you to figure it out.

DARK SOULS: Prepare To Die Edition Hall of Fame 9.5
113.1 hrs · Action RPG / Soulslike · 2012

Same game, revisited. 129 more hours on top of the 113 in the original. The investment across both editions puts this at the same level as DS3 and Skyrim

Skater XL Hall of Fame 8
232.3 hrs · Sports / Skating Sim · 2020

Skateboarding has been one of my great loves. I love the challenge, the process, and the artistic nature of seeing an everyday spot through the lens of how it can be experienced on a skateboard. This game is the purest simulation of that. It's physics-based and you genuinely can't play it without a fundamental understanding of how to manipulate the physics of a skateboard, gravity, and yourself. No score system, no goals, just a skateboarding simulation. There was a time in my life where I skated 4-6 hours every day. My body could only take so much and I was limited by daylight. Skater XL let me continue developing my skate brain when I physically couldn't be out there.
